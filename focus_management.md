# Focus Management

**Question 1**

Deep Work is the ability to focus without distraction on a cognitively demanding task.

**Question 2**

How to Do Deep Work Properly (According to the Author):
1. Work in blocks of time — 60 to 90-minute deep work sessions with breaks.
2. Remove distractions — phone off, no tabs, no notifications.
3. Create a routine — same time/place each day (train your brain to focus).
4. Set clear goals — know exactly what you want to achieve in a session.
5. Embrace boredom — don’t always entertain your mind. It helps you build focus.
6. Prioritize rest — your brain needs recovery to stay productive.

**Question 3**

How can you implement the principles in your day-to-day life?

1. Start small — 30-60 minutes of deep work daily, slowly increase.
2. Use time blocks — schedule specific time windows for focused work.
3. Turn off distractions — silence phone, block websites.
4. Plan your day the night before — include deep work blocks.
5. Use a journal or habit tracker — stay consistent and track your progress.
6. List out works to be done, and check list them by finishing.

**Question 4**

Dangers of Social Media-
1. Addiction & Dopamine Loops - Social media triggers dopamine hits (likes, comments, notifications).
2. Decreased Attention Span - Constant scrolling trains your brain to crave quick stimulation.
3. Mental Health Issues - Increased risk of anxiety, depression, and loneliness, especially in teens.
4. FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) - Creates anxiety about not being part of trends or events.
5. Reduced Real-Life Social Skills - Overreliance on digital communication can weaken in-person social interactions.
